# Sales Psychology Skill

Understand the psychological difference between B2C and B2B messaging so every
funnel speaks the right language to the right buyer.

## Core Principle

| Axis | B2C | B2B |
|------|-----|-----|
| **Primary driver** | Emotion, desire, identity, status | Risk reduction, trust, operational safety |
| **Decision speed** | Fast, impulse-friendly | Slow, multi-stakeholder review |
| **Audience** | Single buyer = user | Finance pays, manager approves, staff uses, IT/security evaluates |
| **Proof type** | Testimonials, social proof, reviews | Case studies, ROI data, demos, compliance docs |
| **Messaging tone** | Aspirational ("feel amazing", "stand out") | Reassuring ("easy deployment", "staff adoption", "low training") |
| **Visual style** | Bold, emotional, lifestyle imagery | Clean, trustworthy, professional |

## B2C: Lead with Emotion

People buy faster based on desire, relief, convenience, status, or excitement.
They justify the purchase with logic afterward.

**Use when messaging:** consumers, solopreneurs, startups buying small tools

**Effective triggers:**
- Fear of missing out (FOMO)
- Identity / self-image ("this is who I am")
- Convenience / time saved
- Status / belonging
- Relief from a painful problem

**Example language:**
- "Finally, the [result] you've been waiting for."
- "Join 10,000+ happy [audience] who already made the switch."
- "Stop [pain]. Start [result]."

## B2B: Lead with Risk Reduction

Companies are asking one thing: *"Will this waste money, break operations, or
make me look bad?"* The buyer is often not the user — finance pays, the manager
approves, staff uses it, IT/security evaluates it. Messaging must work on
every layer.

**Use when messaging:** enterprises, schools, clinics, logistics companies,
SMEs, government, regulated industries

**Effective triggers:**
- Less operational risk
- Staff adoption / ease of training
- Save admin time
- Improve coordination
- Works with existing stack
- Local support available
- ROI proof / case studies

**Example language:**
- "Deploy in days, not months."
- "Built for [industry] — no custom development needed."
- "Reduce support tickets by [X]% with self-service onboarding."
- "Trusted by [similar company] to handle [specific use case]."
- "On-premise or cloud — your choice."

## The Nuance

### B2B Still Uses Emotion

Enterprise software still sells on fear (of being left behind), career
advancement, status, trust, and certainty. Companies like Apple, Notion, and
Slack use design and feeling — even in business markets.

### B2C Still Needs Logic

Expensive consumer purchases (cars, healthcare, education, financial services)
still need logical justification. People backfill emotional decisions with
rational reasons.

### The Funnel Tells You Which

| Funnel type | Default stance |
|-------------|----------------|
| Opt-In (lead magnet) | B2C — emotion to get the click |
| SaaS landing | B2B — trust + risk reduction |
| Webinar | Mix — emotional hook, logical case |
| High-Ticket | B2B — authority + risk reversal |
| E-Commerce | B2C — desire + urgency |
| Membership | B2C — belonging + identity |
| Application | B2B — exclusivity + qualification |

## How to Apply

1. **Ask** who the buyer is — consumer or business?
2. **Identify** the decision chain — one person or a committee?
3. **Lead** with the primary driver (emotion for B2C, risk reduction for B2B)
4. **Layer** the secondary driver underneath (logic under B2C emotion, status/FOMO under B2B safety)
5. **Match** proof type — testimonials for B2C, case studies for B2B
6. **Test** tone with every new funnel type

When in doubt, default to trust + clarity. Both B2C and B2B respond to
honesty and specificity.
